# The client — Nordwind Aerospace

You are walking into this exercise as a **solution architect on a new partner-consultancy engagement**.

Your firm was introduced to Nordwind Aerospace two weeks ago through a warm reference from their chair. Last Thursday you ran a 90-minute discovery call at their Stockholm office. You took scrappy notes. Those notes are in [`meeting-notes.md`](meeting-notes.md). Read them now.

The CTO, Ingrid Aslaug, asked you one thing: **come back with a one-pager the board can read**, answering "can we just use ChatGPT for this, or do we need something more?" — and being specific about what Nordwind should buy, build, or stop doing.

The board meets in ten days. You have about fifteen minutes before your next call to produce a draft worth sending to Ingrid tonight.

Your deliverable is `brief.md` at the repo root, based on the structure in [`brief-template.md`](brief-template.md).

## Ground your brief in real Microsoft guidance

You are going to use the **Microsoft Learn MCP** from Copilot so the brief is grounded in current, verifiable Azure documentation — specifically:

- **Azure Landing Zone for AI** ([aka.ms/aiazurelandingzone](https://aka.ms/aiazurelandingzone))
- **Microsoft Agent Framework** ([learn.microsoft.com/agent-framework](https://learn.microsoft.com/agent-framework))

You do **not** need to know these two topics cold. That is the point of the exercise — Copilot + Learn MCP bring the ground truth. Your job is to frame it for Nordwind.

## What makes a good brief

- **Answers Ingrid's question in the first two sentences.** No preamble.
- **Talks to Nordwind specifically.** "You have 18 satellites and 6 ops engineers" beats "satellite operators typically have…".
- **Three honest next steps.** Not ten. Not "do everything". Specific, sequenced, costed in weeks.
- **Says what not to do.** A consultant who never says "no" is a bad consultant.
